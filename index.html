Introduction

Welcome to the official website for the IEEE PES Task Force on Building Energy Modeling for Flexible Scheduling.

This initiative integrates mainstream learning modeling algorithms, visualizes modeling results on both building and city scale, and provides a standardized workflow bridging building information modeling and power scheduling algorithms.

Background

The building sector accounts for approximately 30%-40% of global energy consumption and carbon emissions. In the transition of building decarbonization, improving the flexibility of energy scheduling and promoting the interaction with power grids will represent a significant role. In the PES Subcommittee on Customer Systems & Smart Buildings, members from different disciplines and domains conducted valuable research and application of flexible scheduling of electricity, heating, cooling, and gas in buildings.

Recent development in deep learning and artificial intelligence has inspired innovative learning-based modeling methods, ranging from physics-informed neural networks to AI agents. A key question is: how can advanced learning methods innovate traditional building modeling fields to support large-scale building-grid interaction?

Objectives

This task force aims to establish a high-quality, fair, and scalable framework that systematically evaluates building modeling methods. Specifically, this task force will:

  • Provide a survey of the technical route on simulation-based building modeling and identify necessary improvement directions.
  • Build a technology repository in advanced learning techniques, ranging from deep learning methods, physics-informed learning, to LLM-powered agents.
  • Review and categorize open-source datasets that support the applications of various learning techniques.
  • Build use cases in real-world buildings to evaluate the scalable potential of learning-enabled techniques for flexible energy scheduling.
